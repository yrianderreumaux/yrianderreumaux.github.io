About Me

I am a Social Psychology PhD Candidate at the University of California, Riverside. My research lies at the intersection of social psychology and political science. I use a variety of methods to tackle open-ended research problems, including experimental design, statistical analysis, and computational modeling.

I am also a co-founder of a social cooking app – Zesty – where I work on all things involving data, including backend development, user experience, and data science.

Design

One of the most important steps in addressing research questions is to carefully consider what methods are needed to generate relevant data, be that querying a database, designing a survey, or conducting A/B tests. With a background in social psychological research, I use my extensive experience to design research methods tailored to each context.

Process

With data in hand, the next step is choosing the correct analysis pipeline. I have a strong set of skills and qualifications to wrangle and analyze data, including expertise in R, Python, and SQL programming languages. I use my quantitative background to employ state-of-the-art quantitative methods in order to generate important research insights, ranging from linear and nonlinear regressions, multilevel modeling, natural language processing, machine learning,to deep neural networks.

Visualize

A picture says more than a thousand words, and the same is true when it comes to data. I use a variety of data visualization tools, such as ggplot2, Matplotlib, and Tableau to create visually appealing and informative graphics that effectively convey the key findings of my research. I also have excellent communication and presentation skills, which I use to clearly and effectively explain my findings to a variety of audiences, including colleagues, clients, and stakeholders.

Yrian Derreumaux

About

Yrian is a Social Psychology PhD Candidate (Quantitative Analytical Methods Minor) at the University of California, Riverside. He uses mixed methods to create innovative solutions to open-ended research problems, including experimental design, statistical analysis, and machine learning.

Academic research

Yrian's research examines how people search out and use information in their environment to update their beliefs, and how understanding biases in these processes can inform interventions that reduce political impasse and foster collective action. This includes examining how people learn about the impact of gun laws on crime rates, vaccine efficacy against Omicron, and the impacts of climate change on Arctic Sea levels.

ZestyApp

Yrian is one of three co-founders of Zesty, a social cooking app designed to help home cooks create, organize, and share their recipes with their friends and family. Yrian applied the quantitative skills he learned during graduate school to build a recommendation system built using neural networks, in addition to designing several dashboards to track user behavior.

Restaurant Career

Prior to pursuing a career in research, Yrian worked as a chef. Trained at Johnson & Wales University, Yrian went on to work for seven years in high-end restaurants across the bay area, including opening a restaurant -- Wingtip -- in a private club in San Francisco

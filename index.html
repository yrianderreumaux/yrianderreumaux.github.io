About Me

I'm a data-driven scientist with experience in studying how people gather information and update their beliefs. During my Ph.D. in the Social Neuroscience Lab at the University of California, Riverside, I gained expertise in designing research methods, processing and analyzing data, and creating impactful visualizations to drive insightful decisions. My research aims to shed light on how motivations influence behavior, with the goal of informing research design aimed at increasing user engagement and satisfaction.

Research & Data Analysis

I leverage my Social Psychology background and startup experience to craft effective research designs and unravel complex data patterns. My toolkit includes advanced quantitative methods like linear/nonlinear regressions, multilevel modeling, Bayesian analysis, and drift diffusion models. This blend of expertise fuels my ability to generate insights that enhance user experiences and guide strategic decisions

Data Visualization & Communication

My journey as a Quantitative User Experience Researcher has led me to appreciate the importance of making data relatable through visualization. Building on my prior experience as a chef, where clear communication was vital, I strive to present complex findings in an accessible manner. This blend of experiences helps me in fostering understanding and collaboration in teams, aiming to enrich user experiences through informed decisions.
